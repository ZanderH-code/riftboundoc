<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">000.</div><div class="rule-text">Golden Rule</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">001.</div><div class="rule-text">The tournament rules exist to ensure anyone who attends a Riftbound organized play event has a fun, equitable, fair, safe and consistent experience. They aim to empower competitors and judges to work together to have mutual expectations and understandings.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">100.</div><div class="rule-text">Introduction</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">101.</div><div class="rule-text">Purpose: This document provides the frameworks and structures for Riftbound competitions by defining rules, responsibilities and procedures to be followed in all Riftbound competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">102.</div><div class="rule-text">Consistency: All Riftbound competitions must be run consistently regardless of their organizer or location to ensure equal treatment of players and interchangeability of events no matter the region or level.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">103.</div><div class="rule-text">Responsibility:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">103.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Attendees: All competitors AND all competition officials are expected to be responsible for following the rules as written and in the spirit in which they were written. This includes respecting all people at competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">103.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectators: Spectators have their own set of responsibilities and expectations. See 204.8 for more information on spectators.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">103.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties: Individuals who violate the frameworks and structures in this document are subject to penalties at the appropriate Organized Play Level (OPL). See 205 for more information on Organized Play Levels.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">104.</div><div class="rule-text">Precedence:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">104.1.</div><div class="rule-text">vs. Core Rules: In some cases, information in this document may contradict, or provide information not contained in, the Riftbound Core Rules. In all such cases, this document takes precedence for competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">104.2.</div><div class="rule-text">vs. Official Local Language Translations: The English language version of this document will supersede any translation.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">104.3.</div><div class="rule-text">vs. Specific Event Addenda: In some cases, information in this document may be contradicted by alternate or additional policies or procedures in official addenda for specific competitions. In all such cases, those addenda take precedence.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">105.</div><div class="rule-text">Alteration: Riot Games or its official Riftbound partners reserve the right to alter this document, or any subsequent competition-specific addenda, at any time without prior notice.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">200.</div><div class="rule-text">Definitions</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">201.</div><div class="rule-text">Competition Types: Riftbound competitions come in three types.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">201.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Premier: A competition that is run by Riot Games or an official competition organizer and has a unique name and features.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">201.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Qualifier: Any competition where rewards include access or advantages for premier events. (Premier events can themselves be qualifiers.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">201.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Local: Any competition that is neither premier nor qualifier.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">202.</div><div class="rule-text">Competition Format: Riftbound competitions can be run with one of two formats.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Limited: A limited competition is one where all product needed for play is provided during the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Constructed: A constructed competition is one where players compete using decks they prepare beforehand.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Swiss: A competition or component of a competition where players are paired based on their current standing in the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Playoff: A competition or component of a competition where players are initially paired based on their standing and are subsequently eliminated by losses.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Rules: Each format has rules specific to it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">202.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Multiple: Some competitions may consist of multiple formats within the same competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">203.</div><div class="rule-text">Units of Play:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">203.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Game: A Riftbound game is defined in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">203.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Match: A Riftbound match is defined in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">204.</div><div class="rule-text">Competition Roles: Every attendee at a competition has at least one of the following roles.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Official roles: Roles 3-6 are considered competition officials for the purpose of this document.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Multiple: Any individual can act in any combination of official roles.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Judge roles: Roles 4-5 are considered judges for the purpose of this document.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges equally serve as both rules arbiters and ambassadors of a healthy and welcoming environment for all players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Competition Organizer (CO) : The CO is responsible for all competition logistics, including:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Sanctioning the event by registering it with Riot Games or its official partners.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Providing a site for the event (the venue) that meets the competition&#x27;s needs, including providing sufficient space and amenities for the expected attendance.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Promoting the event before it begins.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Staffing the event with the necessary tournament officials and support.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Providing all materials necessary to operate the event.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Reporting the event results.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.3.g.</div><div class="rule-text">Maintaining records of the event results (by game and match) for a minimum period of 3 months for the purpose of appeals. See 413 for more information on appeals.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Head Judge: All sanctioned competitions require a Head Judge. The Head Judge is the final authority on competition rules and regulations, and their responsibilities include:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Ensuring that rules, procedures and regulations are followed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Ensuring that all game or policy violations are dealt with and any appropriate corrective action taken.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Responding to floor judges who bring matters to their attention.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Coordinating and delegating responsibilities to floor judges as necessary.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Determining corrective action for errors or violations of the scorekeeper or floor judges.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.4.f.</div><div class="rule-text">The head judge has ultimate discretion if situations arise where the written rules are unclear or inadequate.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Floor Judge: Floor judges respond to players and spectators who have questions about rules and regulations, and/or need judgment on the same, and their responsibilities include:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Answering questions about rules, interactions among cards, providing Watcher wordings, or derived game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.5.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, judges may not answer direct questions about the results of game actions a player has not yet taken. In these situations, players must ask hypothetical questions about the rules. Examples:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">204.5.a.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Inappropriate question: &quot;If I attack and conquer this battlefield with my Tryndamere, Barbarian, will I score the winning point?&quot;</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">204.5.a.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Appropriate question: &quot;Can point scoring abilities like Tryndamere&#x27;s score you the winning point?&quot;</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">204.5.a.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges may ask players to rephrase their questions to be appropriate where possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Provide rulings related to game and policy violations brought to their attention.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Assist with translation if able.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.5.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Assist players in technically precise play (at low OPL). (This should generally not include strategic advice.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Scorekeeper: All sanctioned competitions require a scorekeeper. Scorekeepers are responsible for correct competition logistics as it relates to games and game records, including:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Generating each round of the competition and necessary data to proceed with that round (e.g. match pairings).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.6.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Generating standings in the competition before the last round of the competition (in Swiss competitions).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.6.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Generating standings in the competition after the last round of the competition (in Swiss competitions).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.6.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Solving scorekeeping problems with a-c above in conjunction with the Head Judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.6.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Reporting on the competition afterward and including all necessary data.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Player</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Any non-official participant of the competition is a player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players have responsibilities, including:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Behaving respectfully to all attendees of the competition, including officials and spectators.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Maintaining clear game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Complying with all processes and procedures of the competition, including announcements and time limits.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Calling to the attention of a judge any rules or policy violation in their matches.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Calling to the attention of a judge any error in their competition record as noted by the scorekeeper.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Reporting the result of their competition matches to an official in a timely manner.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Obeying eligibility rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Familiarizing themselves with this document and any addenda.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.b.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Presenting themselves physically for the competition for its duration.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.7.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Even if officials assist, the player remains ultimately responsible for the above.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.7.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Individual members of a team in a team competition are each individually responsible for the above.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.7.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Teammates are responsible for calling to the attention of a judge any rules or policy violations they observe in each others&#x27; matches.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.7.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Violations of the above responsibilities may be subject to penalties, suspension, or revocation of that player&#x27;s eligibility.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">204.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectator</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Any person present at a competition but not in one of the above roles is a spectator.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.b.</div><div class="rule-text">A player who is at a competition but not currently playing a match is also a spectator.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectators have responsibilities, including:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Remaining silent during matches and anywhere else in the venue where silence is required.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.c.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At Casual and Competitive OPL, spectators can ask players to pause play while they alert a judge of a potential issue.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.c.3.</div><div class="rule-text">At Professional OPL, spectators can&#x27;t ask players to pause play, but they can still alert a judge of a potential issue.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectator are prohibited from the following:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Personally indicating rules or policy violations to players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Providing strategic advice to players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.d.3.</div><div class="rule-text">At Professional OPL, spectators may not ask players to pause play while they call a matter to a judge&#x27;s attention.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may request to a judge that a spectator not observe their match, at that judge&#x27;s discretion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.e.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t request this for strategic reasons.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">204.8.e.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At non-local competitions, there may be additional rules for spectator behavior and presence.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">204.8.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Competition officials may instruct that a spectator not observe a match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">205.</div><div class="rule-text">Organized Play Level (OPL): OPL is shorthand that helps communicate expectations to players and judges.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">205.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The purpose of OPL is to define how strictly to treat deviations from rules, policy, and procedures as violations and what penalty those violations warrant.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">205.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Casual (low): Most competitions are casual and are focused on fun and social interaction, not rules enforcement.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are expected to know most of the rules as defined in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are responsible for playing according to the rules in the Core Rules and this document, but:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">205.2.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The focus of judges and competition officials is on education and sportsmanship.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">205.2.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">There is no priority placed on technically precise play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Generally, violations at this level have lower penalties which are covered below in section 700.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">205.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Competitive (high): These competitions have significant rewards, which may include invitations or advantages at the Professional level.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are expected to know the rules as defined in the Core Rules and this document.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">All attendees are expected to maintain the integrity of the competition, while recognizing that some players may not be used to playing at the highest and most technically precise level of play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.3.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Generally, violations at this level have stricter penalties which are covered below in section 700.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">205.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Professional (high): These competitions have significant rewards and prestige.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are expected to know the rules as defined in the Core Rules and this document completely.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">All attendees are expected to maintain the integrity of the competition and the highest standard of behavior and technically precise play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">205.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Generally, violations at this level have the strictest penalties which are covered below in section 700.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">206.</div><div class="rule-text">Round Cycle: A round cycle is the duration (in turns) that contains the standard turn for all players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">206.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Additional turns do not affect the round cycle.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">300.</div><div class="rule-text">Eligibility</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">301.</div><div class="rule-text">Anyone is eligible to participate as a player in Riftbound competitions, unless the player is:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Suspended.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A competition official in that same competition, unless the competition explicitly allows it (non-premier competitions only).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Specifically prohibited by Riot or official partner policy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Under 13 years of age and do not have a parent&#x27;s or guardian&#x27;s permission.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Prohibited by law, the rules or policy of the Competition Organizer, or by the venue&#x27;s rules or policy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">301.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Restricted by competition addenda regarding age, as long as those restrictions are messaged ahead of time (at low OPL).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">302.</div><div class="rule-text">Anyone is eligible to participate as a competition official, unless the player is:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">302.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Suspended.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">302.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A player in that same competition, unless the competition explicitly allows it (non-premier competitions only).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">303.</div><div class="rule-text">If a competition official for an event participates in that event, it must be run at Casual OPL.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">304.</div><div class="rule-text">Owners of organizations that run premier competitions may not play in those competitions, even if otherwise allowed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">305.</div><div class="rule-text">Some competitions have additional criteria regarding eligibility for players or competition officials (for example, invitation-only competitions).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id">306.</div><div class="rule-text">Employees of Riot and their families are subject to additional restrictions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Family is defined here as any person living with or related to the relevant employee, but not themselves an employee.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.2.</div><div class="rule-text">All employees and their family are allowed to participate in local competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">306.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If the reward is product or cash, the employee or family member may claim that part of the reward.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Employees must declare themselves to the CO.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The CO may decline to allow Riot employees to participate in the competition for any reason; if so, they must abide by this decision.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.5.</div><div class="rule-text">No Riot employee is allowed to play in premier or qualifier competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">306.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Families of employees are allowed to play in premier or qualifier competitions, if they are otherwise eligible to compete.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">400.</div><div class="rule-text">Policies</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">401.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck Registration</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">401.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players in competitions are required to register their decks and sideboards (if applicable to the format) at high OPL.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">401.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The Head Judge may require deck registration at low OPL.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">401.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A registered decklist must have a complete, unambiguous record of the player&#x27;s Champion Legend, battlefields, Main Deck including Chosen Champion, Rune Deck, and sideboard (if applicable to the format).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">401.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Once a decklist has been registered and delivered to a competition official, it can&#x27;t be changed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">401.4.</div><div class="rule-text">In a constructed event, a player&#x27;s deck must be registered prior to the start of the first round of competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">401.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Other player&#x27;s decklists are normally considered private information. The head judge of an event may run that event or the final rounds of that event using an open decklist policy. In this case, all players&#x27; decklists will be considered public information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">401.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">In an open decklist competition, each round players are provided with their opponent&#x27;s decklist to review at the start of the match and in between games of the match. Players may not review decklists during gameplay.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">401.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Typically, professional OPL competitions should be run with open decklists, but it is ultimately up to the discretion of the head judge regardless of the event level.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">402.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck Size</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">402.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In a constructed event, players must register a Main Deck of exactly 40 cards (including a chosen champion), 1 Legend, 12 runes, and exactly 3 battlefields each with a unique name.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">402.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, deck construction is defined in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">403.</div><div class="rule-text">Sideboard</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In certain competitions, players may have a group of additional cards (outside of their starting configuration) called a sideboard . See 600 for more information on sideboards based on competition formats.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Sideboard size and contents are dictated by the competition format.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Limits on copies of named cards as defined by competition format apply to the combination of Main Deck and sideboard.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may use sideboard cards to modify their deck in between games of a match. Sideboard cards must be exchanged 1 for 1 with Main Deck cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">403.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">During that time, a player may change their Chosen Champion to one from their sideboard or Main Deck that meets the deckbuilding rules of the competition format.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">403.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">In constructed formats, a player may not change their Runes, Legend, or Battlefields at any point after deck registration.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">403.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">After the sideboarding process, a player&#x27;s Main Deck must meet deck size requirements dictated by the competition format.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may not use sideboard cards in the first game of a match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may count the cards in their opponents&#x27; sideboards at any time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.7.</div><div class="rule-text">A player may look at their own sideboard at any time, but must keep it distinguishable from other cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.8.</div><div class="rule-text">A player&#x27;s deck and sideboard must be returned to their registered states before the first game of the next match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.9.</div><div class="rule-text">If a match completely skips the first game due to a penalty, neither player may sideboard for the following game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.10.</div><div class="rule-text">If a game ends in a draw, neither player may sideboard for the following game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">403.11.</div><div class="rule-text">If a game restarts due to an in-game effect or due to corrective action by judges, the state of each player&#x27;s deck and sideboard must remain the same in the restarted game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">404.</div><div class="rule-text">Match</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">404.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A match of Riftbound consists of a series of games that are played until one side has won a predetermined number of games.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">404.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Most matches of Riftbound are &quot;best of 3,&quot; meaning the predetermined number of games to win is 2.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">404.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Games that end in a draw do not count toward this goal.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">404.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If the competition round ends (due to time limits) prior to any side achieving the goal, the winner of the match is the one who has won the most games.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">404.5.</div><div class="rule-text">If all sides have equal game wins, the match is a draw.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">405.</div><div class="rule-text">Start of Match Procedure</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">405.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Prior to the start of each round, players must seek out information about their imminent match, opponent, and seating in the venue.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">405.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must arrive at their seats prior to the start of the round.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">406.</div><div class="rule-text">Start of Game Procedure</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">406.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The setup process for a game of Riftbound is defined in the Core Rules. See CR 110. Setup Process . Additionally:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">In competitions where sideboards are allowed, players may exchange cards from their main deck and their sideboard after each game of a match. See 403 for more information on sideboards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If a game ends in a draw, players must use the same battlefields for the following game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player must shuffle their Main Deck and their Rune Deck. See 420 for more information on shuffling.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player must present their Main Deck, Rune Deck, and sideboard (if applicable to the format) to their opponents.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player may shuffle the Main Deck and Rune Deck of each of their opponents.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.e.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In team competitions, each player may shuffle the Main Deck and Rune Deck of one of their team&#x27;s opponents.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.e.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At Professional OPL, this is required.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.e.3.</div><div class="rule-text">A player may request a judge shuffle their deck in lieu of an opponent, to be honored at judge discretion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.f.</div><div class="rule-text">For the first game of a match, start of game procedures may be performed as soon as both players reach their assigned seat, even before the start of the round clock. Gameplay must wait for the round clock to begin.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.</div><div class="rule-text">For competitions run using a &quot;best of 1&quot; format instead of a &quot;best of 3&quot; format, the head judge may opt to use the altered initial setup process listed below to closer mirror the &quot;best of 3&quot; experience:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player separates their Champion Legend and places it in the Legend Zone.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player randomly selects one of their three Battlefields, and places it into the Battlefield Zone.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players use the Play First Rule listed below to determine the designated player. See 407 for more information on the Play First Rule.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The designated player chooses if they would like to go first or last.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players then have the opportunity to sideboard as if they were in between games of a &quot;best of 3&quot; match. See 403 for more information on sideboards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.6.</div><div class="rule-text">After sideboarding is complete, each player separates their Chosen Champion and places it in the Champion Zone.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">406.1.g.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Players perform shuffling steps 406.1.c.-406.1.e.3. listed above, then proceed to CR 116. Setup Process .</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">407.</div><div class="rule-text">Play First Rule</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">407.1.</div><div class="rule-text">For the first game of a match, a designated player decides whether they wish to play first or last.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">407.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Any random method that is agreed upon by all players in the current game may be used to determine the designated player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">407.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, during playoff matches that occur following the end of Swiss rounds, the higher ranked player from the Swiss rounds is automatically the designated player for the first game of each match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">407.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The player is designated and the choice of playing first is made during the Start of Game procedure. See CR 115. Determine turn order .</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">407.4.</div><div class="rule-text">For games after the first game of a match, the loser of the previous game gets to choose if they play first or last. If the previous game was a draw, the starting play from the previous game is maintained.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">407.5.</div><div class="rule-text">For alterations to this rule for 2v2, see 603.7.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">408.</div><div class="rule-text">End of Match Procedure</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">408.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A match is complete once a match result is recorded by players or a tournament official.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">408.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the time limit for a competition round is reached before a winner is determined, the player whose turn it is finishes their turn.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">After that player&#x27;s turn is complete, three additional turns are played, and then the match ends.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If the game is incomplete at the end of the additional turns, a player is declared the winner of the game if they have a point lead of two or more. If no player has a point lead of two or more, the game is a draw.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Additional turns are untimed, but at professional OPL a judge must be present to ensure a reasonable pace of play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.2.d.</div><div class="rule-text">If the players are between games when the round time ends, no new game is started.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">408.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The winner of a match is the first player who reaches the appropriate game win score.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If neither player has reached that score by the end of the end of match procedure, the player with more game wins wins the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If players are tied on game wins, the match is a draw.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">408.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Single elimination matches use untimed rounds so players can play their games to a natural conclusion. In the event that single elimination rounds must use a timer, the following altered End of Match Procedure can be used to determine a winner if time runs out:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">When time is reached before a winner is determined, if players are in-between games, if a player has more game wins in the match they win the match. If game wins are tied, proceed to 408.4.d.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">When time is reached before a winner is determined, if players are in the middle of a game, the player whose turn it is finishes their turn.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">After that player&#x27;s turn is complete, three additional turns are played. At the conclusion of those additional turns:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">408.4.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player has the most points in the current game, they win that game. Then, if a player has more game wins in the match, they win the match. If the game score is tied, proceed to 408.4.d.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">408.4.c.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the point score of the current game is tied, but a player has the most game wins in the match, that player wins the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">408.4.c.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If the point score of the current game is tied and the game score of the match is tied, players continue to play until the next point is scored. The player who scores that point wins the match. This does not remove normal scoring restrictions if the next point is the Final Point. See CR 448.1.a. for Final Point details.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">408.4.d.</div><div class="rule-text">If the game score is tied and a new game needs to be started, players start the new game and act as though time immediately runs out, beginning the process of 408.4.a., except with five additional turns following the first player&#x27;s turn before moving into sudden death scoring as described in 408.4.c.3. if the point score is tied at the end of those turns.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">409.</div><div class="rule-text">Tiebreakers</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">409.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In the course of determining standings, the scorekeeper might need to distinguish between two players with equal match records.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">409.2.</div><div class="rule-text">In these cases, the following tiebreakers should be applied in order:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">409.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The player with higher opponents&#x27; mean (average) match win percentage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">409.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">The player with higher game win percentage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">409.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">The player with higher opponents&#x27; mean (average) game win percentage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">409.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If after all tiebreakers are applied, two players are still of equal standing in the competition, the scorekeeper should use a random method to break the tie.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">410.</div><div class="rule-text">Concessions and Intentional Draws</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">410.1.</div><div class="rule-text">At any time before a game is completed, any player may concede that game or all players may mutually agree to draw it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">410.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At any time before a match is completed, any player may concede that match or all players may mutually agree to draw it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">410.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t agree to concessions or draws in exchange for anything. See 704.5 for more information on bribery.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">410.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t scout other match results before deciding to concede or draw their match and need to remain in their seats while their match is in progress.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">410.5.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player refuses to play, tournament officials should treat that as conceding the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">411.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck Checks</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">411.1.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, judges should perform deck checks during the course of the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">411.2.</div><div class="rule-text">When deck checks are performed, at least 10% of all decks, chosen at random, should be checked during the course of the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">411.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck checks for the players in a match should occur after players have randomized and presented their decks, but before opening hands are drawn.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">411.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If a deck check is performed within a match, judges should grant a time extension to that match sufficient to cover the logistical time needed to perform the check.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">412.</div><div class="rule-text">Judge Calls</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">412.1.</div><div class="rule-text">At any point during a match, a player may pause to flag a floor judge. See 204.4 for floor judge responsibilities.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">412.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Time extensions should be granted if the total time of a judge call is more than 1 minute.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">413.</div><div class="rule-text">Appeals</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">413.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player disagrees with a judge&#x27;s ruling, they may appeal to the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">413.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, the head judge may designate certain floor judges to be appeals judges, who are then empowered to hear and rule on appeals. In such cases, those judges must wear uniforms matching the head judge&#x27;s uniform.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">413.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may not appeal before a full ruling is made by a judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">413.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may not appeal a ruling of an appeals judge or the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">413.5.</div><div class="rule-text">If a penalty is applied outside the scope of a competition, the appeals process should follow what&#x27;s laid out on the official Riot Games website. [citation needed]</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">414.</div><div class="rule-text">Dropping</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may drop from a competition at any time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player drops before the competition begins, they never participated in the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players who wish to drop must inform the scorekeeper before pairings for the next round of the competition are generated.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player doesn&#x27;t show up for their match, they are automatically dropped from the competition unless they report to the scorekeeper.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players who drop from limited format tournaments own the cards that they correctly have in their possession at the time of dropping, including unopened packs.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.6.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player drops from a competition after a top placement cut has been made, no player is advanced in replacement. The highest ranked remaining player receives a bye if the drop happens before pairings.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Players who have dropped may re-enter the competition before the start of the following round at the discretion of the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t re-enter a competition if they missed a portion where a deck construction activity was required.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">414.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t re-enter a competition after a top placement cut.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">415.</div><div class="rule-text">Tracking Score</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">415.1.</div><div class="rule-text">All players within a game are responsible for tracking all players&#x27; scores throughout the game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">415.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Changes to score should be announced and acknowledged by all players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">415.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Scores must be tracked, displayed, and updated legibly in writing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">415.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, an electronic device, dice, or similar methods can be used to track score.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">415.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If any player notices a score discrepancy, they must immediately make all players aware of it. If it can&#x27;t be resolved easily by the players, a judge must be called.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">416.</div><div class="rule-text">Taking Notes</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">416.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may take written notes during a match and refer to those notes during games in the same match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">416.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At the beginning of each match, each player&#x27;s written note sheet must be empty and visible to all opponents and tournament officials.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">416.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players do not have to reveal their written notes to opponents. Judges may ask players to reveal and/or explain their notes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">416.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may not refer to notes from outside the current match, including notes from previous matches, during games.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">416.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Between games, players may refer to notes they made prior to the match. They do not have to reveal these notes to opponents.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">417.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may use electronic devices during competitions, but can&#x27;t use them during matches.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">417.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, digital devices may take the place of written note sheets.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">417.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The head judge may further restrict or permit usage of electronic devices.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">418.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectators and players who are on camera during matches are responsible for behaving respectfully toward all participants.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">418.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectators are permitted to record matches using video capture, provided they do so without interfering with the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">418.5.</div><div class="rule-text">At Professional OPL, the head judge may consult video capture to help make rulings during a match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">418.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should never honor a player&#x27;s request to consult video capture.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">419.</div><div class="rule-text">Proxies and Alters</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A proxy is a stand-in, unofficial version of an actual Riftbound card.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Proxies are not allowed at competitions of any OPL.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The only exception is an official proxy issued by the head judge at their discretion to replace a card deemed unusable.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.3.</div><div class="rule-text">An alter is an official Riftbound card that has been modified in some way.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Alters are allowed in competition only if the modifications do not:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Modify or obscure the card name or cost, or make the art unrecognizable.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Contain substantial strategic advice.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Contain offensive images or text.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.5.</div><div class="rule-text">By judge discretion, alters may not be allowed to be used during matches that are streamed or otherwise broadcast.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players using altered cards are responsible for having official unaltered versions of those cards to use during gameplay that is streamed or otherwise broadcast if instructed by a judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">When instructed, players must replace the altered versions with the official unaltered versions before gameplay begins for a match that is streamed or otherwise broadcast.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">419.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Alters containing material outside of Riot Games intellectual property may not be permitted during matches that are streamed or otherwise broadcast.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">419.6.</div><div class="rule-text">The head judge of a competition is the final decider on whether a card is acceptable for a competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">420.</div><div class="rule-text">Card Language</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">420.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may include official Riftbound cards of any language in their decks during deckbuilding and gameplay.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">420.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A player may call a judge at any time to get the Watcher wording for any card in the language of their preference.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">421.</div><div class="rule-text">Shuffling</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Decks must be made random at the start of each game, and whenever an instruction from cards or tournament officials require it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Random is defined as a state where no player could have any information about the order or position of cards in any part of the deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Shuffling by methodically placing cards into distinct piles (informally known as &quot;pile shuffling&quot;) may be used as a method to count the deck, but can&#x27;t solely be used to make a deck random.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.4.</div><div class="rule-text">When a deck is made random, it is presented to the player&#x27;s opponent. This signals that the player believes their deck is legal and random.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must notify a judge if they believe their opponents&#x27; deck has not been made sufficiently random. See 703.7 for more information on insufficient randomization.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may shuffle their opponents&#x27; decks additionally.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must take care not to damage cards or sleeves through this process of shuffling, nor cause cards or sleeves to become marked.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">421.8.</div><div class="rule-text">When two or more cards are recycled to the player&#x27;sMain Deck as part of a single action, the above procedures 1-7 should be followed for that group of cards to properly randomize them before recycling them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">422.</div><div class="rule-text">Sleeves</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may use plastic sleeves or other protective devices on their cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player chooses to do this, all such sleeves must be identical and all cards in a deck must be placed in sleeves in the same manner. Cards in decks must have the same number of layers and be the same orientation at all times.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">422.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">It&#x27;s suggested but not required that players use different sleeves for the different decks (Main, Rune).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">422.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may use a unique sleeve or additional layer to distinguish their Chosen Champion during game setup, but must change its sleeve to match their Main Deck if it would enter a zone whose information level is private during gameplay. See CR 127. Privacy for information types.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Sleeves must have opaque backs.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.4.</div><div class="rule-text">During a match, a player may request that a judge inspect an opponent&#x27;s sleeves.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges may disallow any specific sleeve or the sleeves generally if they believe they are marked, worn, inappropriate, offensive, or otherwise interfere with shuffling or play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.6.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, using sleeves for the Main Deck, Rune Deck and Battlefields is a requirement. At low OPL, using sleeves can be required at the discretion of the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">422.7.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, sleeves must also not:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">422.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Have highly reflective backs.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">422.7.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Contain holographic patterns on the front or back.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">423.</div><div class="rule-text">Marked Cards</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">423.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are responsible during competitions to make sure their cards and/or sleeves are not marked.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">423.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Marked is defined as bearing something that makes it possible to identify the card without seeing its face.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">423.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If cards are sleeved, they should be inspected for marking while in the sleeves.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">423.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges may ask players to remove sleeves or replace any specific sleeve at any time during a competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">424.</div><div class="rule-text">Display</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">424.1.</div><div class="rule-text">During a match, the start of match procedure, and other relevant times during a competition, players must keep cards above the vertical level of the playing surface.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">424.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are responsible for making reasonable effort to prevent secret information from being revealed accidentally. See CR 127. Privacy for information types.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">424.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may reveal private information unless specifically prohibited by the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">500.</div><div class="rule-text">Communication</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">501.</div><div class="rule-text">Requirements</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">501.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are required to communicate honestly about the state of the game with their opponents.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">501.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are required to communicate honestly and completely with tournament officials.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">501.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are not required to assist their opponents in playing the game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">501.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are required to treat other players, tournament officials, and spectators with respect.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">501.5.</div><div class="rule-text">In team events, communication between teammates is allowed and encouraged, but it must be done in a reasonably timely manner. See 703.2 for more information on Slow Play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">502.</div><div class="rule-text">Information</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Cards have a level of privacy as specified in CR 127. Privacy . These apply to levels of information accessible to the players as well.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.2.</div><div class="rule-text">In addition to these levels of information, some information is derived.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Derived information is a combination of public information and a player&#x27;s own skill or calculation.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are not required to assist opponents in determining derived information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Like cards, other relevant information about the game state can be public, derived, private, or secret.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The additional relevant public information includes:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The current turn player, phase, step, state of the turn, and who has priority and focus.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Rune Pools and the number and quantity of resources in them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">All players&#x27; current scores.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.d.</div><div class="rule-text">A permanent&#x27;s current state, as distinct from its printed ones.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Spells and abilities on the Chain, their order, and play choices made for them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.4.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Spells and abilities that resolved previously in the turn, and the sources of them are themselves public.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.5.</div><div class="rule-text">The additional relevant derived information includes:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Which public cards have been recycled.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">The order of runes in a Rune Deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">502.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Consequences of actions that might happen in the future, such as a unit receiving a Might bonus from the Shield ability if a player attacks.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must acknowledge changes to public information that result from their cards and effects as those effects would have an observable impact on the game. See 506.3.d. for examples of observable impact.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must answer honestly any questions regarding public information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must not represent public or derived information incorrectly.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must point out discrepancies between announced and recorded public information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.10.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges are encouraged to help players determine public information, but not derived information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">502.11.</div><div class="rule-text">In 2v2, teammates may share Private information, including directly showing each other the cards in their hand as defined by 484.8.e. in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">503.</div><div class="rule-text">Shortcuts</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In competitions, players may take actions that skip parts of the technically precise play sequence without explicitly announcing that sequence.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.2.</div><div class="rule-text">The most common type of shortcut is skipping one or more parts of a turn, phase, or step to the mutual understanding of all involved players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may request to use a new shortcut in a game but must be clear about the game state at the start and end of the shortcut.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may interrupt shortcuts by explaining where they want to intervene to take an action.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may interrupt their own shortcuts.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t use shortcuts they haven&#x27;t previously announced or modify existing shortcuts without announcement.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t interrupt a shortcut with the intent to do nothing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.8.</div><div class="rule-text">When resolving spells or abilities, players can&#x27;t assume their opponents are using shortcuts without asking.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">503.9.</div><div class="rule-text">The following are common shortcuts for Riftbound competitions:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">503.9.a.</div><div class="rule-text">When a player channels runes or draws a card at the start of their turn, it is assumed that they have passed through the Beginning Phase.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">503.9.b.</div><div class="rule-text">When a player places a spell or ability on the chain, they are assumed to be passing priority unless they explicitly announce they intend to retain it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">503.9.c.</div><div class="rule-text">When a player puts a spell or ability on the chain and announces choices for it that are normally made upon resolution, they must adhere to those choices unless their opponents react.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">503.9.d.</div><div class="rule-text">When a player asks about choices normally made upon resolution, they are assumed to be passing priority and allowing the spell or ability to resolve.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">504.</div><div class="rule-text">Sequencing</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">504.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Complex series of game actions are sometimes shortcutted into out-of-order sequences, which are technically incorrect or imprecise, but arrive at the correct legal and understood game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">504.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: Drawing a card before channeling runes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">504.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may ask their opponents to do these actions, or a subset of these actions, in the correct order so that they can react at the appropriate time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">504.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can&#x27;t shortcut out-of-order in a way that gives them information prematurely that might affect decisions later in that sequence.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">504.4.</div><div class="rule-text">When something is recycled, it must be immediately put on the bottom of the appropriate deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">504.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: A player may not keep their recycled runes facedown on the board to track Energy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">504.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Failure to recycle in this way is a Communication Violation. See 703.5 for more information on Communication Violations.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">505.</div><div class="rule-text">Loops</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Some sequences of actions can be repeated and are shortcutted as a loop.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players performing a loop must have each iteration of the loop be identical with no conditional actions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.3.</div><div class="rule-text">To decide how to proceed with a loop, determine how many players are needed to maintain it (who must take actions or make decisions to sustain the loop):</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">505.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">No players</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">505.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">One player</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">505.3.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Two or more players</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.4.</div><div class="rule-text">For each maintaining player (if any), in turn order, that player chooses a number of iterations.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Each other player (if any), in turn order, may choose to agree to that number or choose a number of iterations after which they commit to taking action to break the loop or choose not to break the loop.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.6.</div><div class="rule-text">If no player chooses to break the loop and there were no maintaining players, the game ends in a draw.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, the loop continues until the lowest number of iterations chosen, then that player either gains priority (if a maintaining player) or breaks the loop.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.8.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player intervenes to break a loop, they may choose to interrupt a partially completed iteration.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.9.</div><div class="rule-text">If a sequence of actions is non-deterministic, it may not be shortcut and iterations must be performed manually.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.10.</div><div class="rule-text">Some loops are maintained by decisions rather than actions. In these cases, apply 505.4-505.8 but with the player making a different decision rather than a different action.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.11.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges are the final arbiter of what constitutes a loop, or if choices are available to continue a loop when secret information is involved.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">505.12.</div><div class="rule-text">Players who try to opt-out of shortcutting or propose incorrect shortcutting to use up time are cheating. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">506.</div><div class="rule-text">Triggered Abilities</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">506.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Accountable Player: The player responsible for putting a triggered ability on the chain, as defined by the core rules. See CR 375. Triggered Abilities for more information on triggered abilities.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The accountable player for triggers sourced from battlefields depends on control of the battlefield and is not automatically the player who brought the battlefield to the game. See CR 184.6. For more information on battlefield abilities.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">506.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players are not required to notify other players of triggered abilities when they are not the accountable player, but may if they wish.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, a player is not required to notify other players of triggered abilities when they are not the accountable player, but it is encouraged for sportsmanship and learning.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">506.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The accountable player must acknowledge their triggers by the time they would have an observable impact on the game. If they do not, they are forgotten.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A trigger is considered forgotten once its point of observable impact has been reached and a new game action is taken without the accountable player acknowledging the trigger.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players ruled to be abusing this rule to intentionally forget mandatory triggers that they are the accountable player for will be penalized. See 702.2 for more information about missed triggers as a penalty.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.3.c.</div><div class="rule-text">For triggers that occur &quot;At the start of each player&#x27;s first Beginning Phase&quot;, the accountable player has the full duration of the turn in which the trigger occurs to acknowledge the trigger before it&#x27;s considered forgotten, regardless of its observable impact. (Example cards: Obelisk of Power and The Arena&#x27;s Greatest.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples of observable impact:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The trigger would change a point total. (Example card: Ahri, Alluring)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">The trigger would change a rune total. (Example card: Startipped Peak)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The trigger would add a Buff token to a unit. (Example card: Sett, Brawler)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The trigger would impact combat or other game procedures. A card that reads &quot;When you play a spell, give me +1 Might this turn.&quot; does NOT have an immediate observable impact. It has an observable impact once it&#x27;s Might would be relevant. (Example card: Ravenbloom Student)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.5.</div><div class="rule-text">A trigger would cause a player to draw or discard. (Example card: Traveling Merchant)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.6.</div><div class="rule-text">A trigger would cause a game object to be exhausted or readied. (Example card: Miss Fortune, Captain)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.7.</div><div class="rule-text">A trigger would cause a unit to move. (Example card: Stealthy Pursuer)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.8.</div><div class="rule-text">A player asks for public information that would be impacted by a trigger. See 502 for more information on public information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">506.3.d.9.</div><div class="rule-text">A player would be required to make a choice of whether or not to place an optional trigger on the chain. (Example: Overzealous Fan)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">506.4.</div><div class="rule-text">A triggered ability that was forgotten never goes on the chain.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">506.5.</div><div class="rule-text">A triggered ability that was forgotten is still considered to have triggered for the purpose of non-optional &quot;First time&quot; or similarly restricted triggers.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">507.</div><div class="rule-text">Teams in Separate Matches</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">507.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may communicate with teammates at all times unless explicitly prohibited by the format rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">507.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Players that acquire secret or private information about a match in which a teammate is playing can&#x27;t communicate with that teammate for the remainder of that match. See CR 127. Privacy for information types</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">508.</div><div class="rule-text">Layout</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In high OPL, players must arrange their cards according to the following rules, for clarity.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Runes must be closer to the player than nonrunes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Nonrunes must be closer to the player&#x27;s opponent than the player&#x27;s runes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Main Deck and trash must be together on either the left side or the right side of the board.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Rune Deck should be on the other side (right or left) of the board from the Main Deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Legend must be between nonrunes and Chosen Champion, and both legend and Chosen Champion must be together on either the left side or the right side of the board.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Each card or indicator that attaches to another card must be clearly associated with it physically.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Tokens must be represented by card objects that show all relevant information including ready state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Buffs must be represented by an object that unambiguously represents a buff in game terms.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">508.10.</div><div class="rule-text">Ready objects must be oriented facing the controller. Exhausted objects must be oriented at a 90 degree rotation to ready objects, either clockwise or counterclockwise, but all exhausted objects must be rotated consistently.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">509.</div><div class="rule-text">Gameplay Decisions</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.1.</div><div class="rule-text">In competitions, players are responsible for considering options before taking actions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Once an action is communicated to an opponent, that action can&#x27;t generally be taken back.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.3.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, a player can reverse their most recent action and make a different decision for that action (including not taking it at all) as long as no new action has been taken by either player. The change in decision does not need to be immediate.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.4.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, a player can reverse their most recent action and make a different decision for that action (including not taking it at all) if no new action has been taken by either player and the following is true:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">509.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The player wants to make a different decision for a spell or ability immediately after all choices have been made and communicated for putting it onto the chain. Example:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">509.4.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A player plays Discipline indicating a unit. They immediately change their mind and choose a different unit, or choose not to play the Discipline at all.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">509.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">The player wants to make a different decision for playing a unit or gear immediately after playing it to a location. Example:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">509.4.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A player plays Traveling Merchant to Reaver&#x27;s Row. They immediately change their mind and choose to play it to their base instead, or choose not to play it at all.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">509.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">The player wants to make a different decision immediately after making a choice such as moving a unit to a location or discarding a card. Example:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">509.4.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A player moves Pouty Poro to Void Gate. They immediately change their mind and choose to move an additional unit there as well, or they decide not to move at all.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">509.4.d.</div><div class="rule-text">The player wants to undo a decision to exhaust or recycle runes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">509.4.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A player exhausts two Calm runes. Before taking a new game action, the player decides they do not want to exhaust them, and readies them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Intentionally attempting to reverse decisions to gain information from an opponent is considered angle shooting. Players may not change their decisions based on an opponent&#x27;s physical reaction or as a response to an opponent starting to take a game action. See 704.4. for more information on angle shooting.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">509.6.</div><div class="rule-text">While changing decisions as described above is allowed, players must still maintain an appropriate pace of play. See 703.2 for more information on Slow Play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.3.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">&quot;Blank&quot; battlefields function as battlefields that do not have any rules text. Otherwise, they adhere to all other normal battlefield rules as defined in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.3.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, &quot;Blank&quot; battlefields can be represented by anything agreed upon by all players in the game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">602.3.e.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, players must register a starting configuration from the card pool provided and must register the remaining contents of their card pool as their sideboard.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.3.e.3.</div><div class="rule-text">For &quot;best of 1&quot; competitions, players must register three battlefields to randomly select from during the start of game procedure, including any &quot;blank&quot; battlefields they wish to use.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.3.e.4.</div><div class="rule-text">For &quot;best of 3&quot; competitions, players may select any battlefield from their card pool or a &quot;blank&quot; battlefield during the start of game procedure.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">602.3.f.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, starting configuration registration is not required and players may freely change the composition of their starting deck and sideboard between games and matches unless otherwise specified by the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">602.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Limited Formats</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Sealed</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Sealed is a limited format where players are each provided with 6 unopened Riftbound booster packs to open and use to construct their deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Main Decks for sealed decks must be at least 25 cards, but can be more.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The domain identity of a sealed deck is any three domains, or any domain plus the domains of the deck&#x27;s Champion Legend.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A sealed deck may have any Champion Unit that is included in its domain identity as its Chosen Champion, even one that does not have a champion tag that matches its Champion Legend (if it has one).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">A sealed deck may contain any signature spells that are fully included in its domain identity, even if the deck does not include the corresponding champion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.3.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: A sealed deck with a domain identity of Fury/Body/Order could use signature spells that are Fury/Order or Body/Order, but not signature spells that are Fury/Mind or Body/Chaos.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may only include Runes from any source in their Rune Deck that match the domain identity of their sealed deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.5.</div><div class="rule-text">A sealed deck is still legal if it has no Champion Legend or Chosen Champion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A player may opt not to use a Champion Legend or a Chosen Champion in their sealed deck even if they have one in their provided card pool.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player&#x27;s deck is missing a Champion Legend and/or a Chosen Champion, they draw a card at the start of their first Beginning Phase each game. (If both are missing, they still only draw one card.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.6.</div><div class="rule-text">The normal restrictions on quantities of named or signature cards do not apply. For example, a player may use 10 Pouty Poro in their Main Deck if they received them in the provided card pool.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The &quot;Unique&quot; keyword does not apply in sealed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.7.</div><div class="rule-text">A sealed deck&#x27;s sideboard is every card provided to the player as part of the competition that isn&#x27;t used in their starting deck configuration, unless otherwise specified. See 403 for more information on sideboards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Additionally, players may change their domain identity, Rune Deck, Legend, and Chosen Champion while sideboarding as long as the resulting deck still meets 602.4.a.2-602.4.a.4.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Sealed Pool Registration &amp; Deck Construction</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, the head judge must require registration of the sealed pool prior to deck construction.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player receives the unopened product at their seat.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Players on one side of the table (A) open their product, observed by players on the other side (B).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Players verify that the product has the correct undamaged quantity of cards and rarity distribution. If a pack with an error is found, call a judge to replace the pack if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Once all unopened product has been opened and observed by B, those cards are placed next to B.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Repeat the steps outlined in 602.4.8.c-e but with (B) opening and (A) observing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.g.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player now sorts and registers the contents of the pool placed next to them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.h.</div><div class="rule-text">After registration is complete, each player returns the pool and registration sheet to the other player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.i.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player verifies that their own pool has been registered correctly.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.j.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck construction begins.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.k.</div><div class="rule-text">It&#x27;s recommended that players be given 20 minutes for deck registration and 30 minutes for deck construction. Deck construction can be increased to 45 minutes for low OPL events where players are experiencing the cards for the first time, such as pre-rift and release events.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.8.l.</div><div class="rule-text">The starting deck configuration and sideboard are registered on the same sheet as the total card pool.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Preconstructed Decks in Sealed Competitions</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.9.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Some competitions or portions of competitions provide preconstructed decks for players to use. These are considered sealed decks.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.9.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must use exactly the decks provided, except as modified by any addenda to the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.a.9.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">This may override the sealed deck construction rules above.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Draft</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Draft is a limited format where players are each provided with 3 unopened Riftbound booster packs to open and draft amongst other players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Draft groups consist of as close to eight players as possible, seated randomly around a table. Draft process is as follows:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player opens one of their boosters, removes the Rune or token, then looks through the remaining contents to ensure there are thirteen undamaged cards in the pack with a correct rarity distribution.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players can keep the Rune or token they opened.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If an opened pack has an error, players must call a judge to replace the pack if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Once pack contents are verified, players will look through the cards in their pack and select one to draft to the top of a single unobscured facedown pile in front of them, then pass the remaining contents facedown to the player to their left. Players repeat this process with the cards passed to them until there are no cards remaining.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Once the first pack is completed, repeat 602.4.b.2.a-602.4.b.2.b with the second pack, except passing cards to the right.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Once the second pack is completed, repeat 602.4.b.2.a-602.4.b.2.b with the third pack, again passing cards to the left.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Once a card has been added to a player&#x27;s single facedown pile in front of them it is considered selected and can&#x27;t be returned to the pack.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must make a reasonable effort to conceal the front face of cards they have selected and cards in their current pack from other players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Once this process is complete, each player should have 39 cards to use as their limited card pool.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.d.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, players may not use electronic devices to take notes or as a strategic aid during drafting. At low OPL, this can be allowed by the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.e.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, players may not receive any outside assistance during drafting. At low OPL, this can be allowed by the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.f.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, during the draft process players may not reveal cards they have seen or selected, or openly discuss the draft. At low OPL, this can be allowed by the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.g.</div><div class="rule-text">During the draft process, players are not permitted to seek knowledge of cards fronts other drafters are viewing or have already selected through any means. Attempting to do so is cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.g.1.</div><div class="rule-text">It is not cheating for a player to see card backs of cards other players are viewing or of cards players have added to the facedown pile in front of them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.h.</div><div class="rule-text">At high OPL, players can&#x27;t look at the cards they have already drafted, except during intervals designated by a judge between packs. At low OPL, this is allowed at any time as long as the player is not holding other cards at the same time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.2.i.</div><div class="rule-text">At professional OPL, drafts must be timed. At competitive OPL, drafts are timed at the discretion of the head judge. At casual OPL, drafts are untimed but players must draft at a reasonable pace.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Main Decks for draft decks must be at least 20 cards, but can be more.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The domain identity of a draft deck is any three domains, or any domain plus the domains of the deck&#x27;s Champion Legend.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A draft deck may have any Champion Unit that is included in its domain identity as its Chosen Champion, even one that does not have a champion tag that matches its Champion Legend (if it has one).</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">A draft deck may contain any signature spells that are fully included in its domain identity, even if the deck does not include the corresponding champion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.4.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: A draft deck with a domain identity of Fury/Body/Order could use signature spells that are</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-0"><div class="rule-id"></div><div class="rule-text">Fury/Order or Body/Order, but not signature spells that are Fury/Mind or Body/Chaos.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may only include Runes from any source in their Rune Deck that match the domain identity of their draft deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.6.</div><div class="rule-text">A draft deck is still legal if it has no Champion Legend or Chosen Champion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A player may opt not to use a Champion Legend or a Chosen Champion in their draft deck even if they have them among their drafted cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.6.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player&#x27;s deck is missing a Champion Legend and/or a Chosen Champion, they draw a card at the start of their first Beginning Phase each game. (If both are missing, they still only draw one card.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.7.</div><div class="rule-text">The normal restrictions on quantities of named or signature cards do not apply. For example, a player may use 10 Pouty Poro in their Main Deck if they selected them during the draft.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The &quot;Unique&quot; keyword does not apply in draft.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.8.</div><div class="rule-text">A draft deck&#x27;s sideboard is every card drafted by the player as part of the competition that isn&#x27;t used in their starting deck configuration, unless otherwise specified. See 403 for more information on sideboards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">602.4.b.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Additionally, players may change their domain identity, Rune Deck, Legend, and Chosen Champion while sideboarding as long as the resulting deck still meets 602.4.b.3-602.4.b.5.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">603.</div><div class="rule-text">2v2</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A 2v2 team consists of 2 players who enter a competition together and register together as a team to play congruently in a single match as described in the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Each player must provide their individual registration information to enter the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">603.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">When registering, teams must specify an ordering for the players on their team for seating purposes by indicating an A player and a B player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player drops from a competition, their entire team is dropped.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player is disqualified from a competition, their entire team is disqualified.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.5.</div><div class="rule-text">In some competitions, 2v2 teams may provide a team name to be displayed. Tournament officials may disallow a specific name for any reason.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.6.</div><div class="rule-text">A 2v2 competition can be either constructed or sealed. In sealed 2v2, the team shares a single product pool of 8 packs.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">603.7.</div><div class="rule-text">During matches, teammates sit next to each other in their registered AB order. The setup process for a game of Riftbound is defined in the Core Rules, with these additional exceptions for 2v2:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Turn order is as follows:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The first team&#x27;s A player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">The second team&#x27;s A player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">The first team&#x27;s B player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">The second team&#x27;s B player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.a.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Return to the top of the order.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">603.7.b.</div><div class="rule-text">A random method is used to decide which team selects the starting player.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">603.7.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Once the appropriate step in the core rules process is reached, the team that earned the decision chooses where within the a.1-4 turn order play begins.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">603.7.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">For games after the first game of a match, the losing team of the previous game gets to choose who plays first. If the previous game was a draw, the turn order from the previous game is maintained.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">604.</div><div class="rule-text">Event and Round Time Limits</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">604.1.</div><div class="rule-text">It&#x27;s recommended that players be given 60 minutes for rounds of play in the Swiss portion of a competitive event.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">604.2.</div><div class="rule-text">It is strongly recommended that single elimination matches use untimed rounds so players can play their games to a natural conclusion. If single elimination rounds must be timed, see 408.4. for an altered End of Match procedure.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-chapter level-0"><div class="rule-id">700.</div><div class="rule-text">Enforcement and Penalties</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">701.</div><div class="rule-text">General</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Philosophy</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors are assumed to be committed unintentionally. If the judge believes it was intentional, it is cheating. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.1.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players attempting to abuse error remedies to receive advantages are cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges are neutral arbiters and enforcers of policy and rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should not stop errors from occurring. Instead they deal with errors that have occurred.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should not intervene in games unless:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.1.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">They believe a violation has occurred.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.1.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A player requested assistance.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.1.d.3.</div><div class="rule-text">They observe a situation related to Unsporting Conduct.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should model fair play, sporting conduct, and diplomatic behavior.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should not account for a player&#x27;s history or skill when determining whether errors occurred.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.g.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges may take into account a player&#x27;s history or skill when investigating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.h.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties are designed to educate players on mistakes and encourage them not to make them in the future.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.i.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties are also designed to deter other players from similar errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.j.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties are issued and tracked to help judges understand intent over patterns of behavior.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.k.</div><div class="rule-text">Head judges and head judges ONLY may issue penalties outside of these guidelines. They have discretion to apply harsher penalties or investigate cheating if needed. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.l.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges are human and they make mistakes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.m.</div><div class="rule-text">If a judge recognizes their own mistakes, they should acknowledge them and correct them if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.n.</div><div class="rule-text">If a competition official makes a mistake and it results in a penalty applied to a player, the head judge can downgrade the penalty.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.1.o.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player acts on information given in error by a competition official, the head judge can rewind the game to account for it, even if there were no other errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Definitions of Penalties</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Warning: An indication that an error occurred that provides a record of the error.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Time extensions should be granted if the total time to rule and correct is more than 1 minute.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Game Loss: A game loss causes the erroring player and any of their teammates to concede the game. If this does not result in a winner, remaining players continue the game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Game losses are applied immediately if the game is ongoing, or to the player&#x27;s next game in the competition if it is not.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player would receive multiple game losses at the same time, they only receive one.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If all players in a match receive a game loss simultaneously, the current game ends in a draw. All game losses are recorded but do not affect the match score.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Match Loss: A severe penalty that&#x27;s issued when the entire match has been compromised by the error.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Match losses are applied to the current match, or to the player&#x27;s next match in the competition if their previous match has ended.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Disqualification: A penalty issued for errors that damage the integrity of the competition or for severe misconduct.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Disqualification causes that player to lose the current match and be dropped from the competition.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player has already physically received prizes from the competition, they keep them, but they will not receive any other prizes after disqualification, even if their placement in the event would otherwise entitle them to prizes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.3.</div><div class="rule-text">When a player is disqualified, they are removed from the competition and from standings.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Any attendee of a competition can be issued a disqualification, not just players. Being barred from the tournament venue may apply to disqualification at the head judge&#x27;s discretion.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.2.d.5.</div><div class="rule-text">This penalty can be issued by the head judge even without conclusive proof if the head judge determines the integrity of the competition has been compromised.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Applying Penalties</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties of Game Loss of higher (b-d) should be reported to the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges should explain the error, the procedure to fix it, and the penalties for involved players.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.3.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Some errors include remedies beyond the scope of the base penalty, to protect competition officials from accusations of bias.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.3.d.</div><div class="rule-text">If a single error leads to multiple related penalties, judges should issue only the one with the most severe penalty.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Shuffling</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Some remedies require shuffling the randomized portion of the deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Many cards recycle cards to the bottom of a deck. The judge should do what they can using information available from the game state and from the players to determine which portion of the deck is knowable by one or more players, and separate those before shuffling that deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.4.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Once the deck has been shuffled, any cards set aside in this way should be returned to their proper locations in the deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.4.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Shuffles performed by a judge in this way are not considered shuffles in game terms.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Rewinding</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Some remedies allow rewinding the game state in extreme circumstances, where leaving the game in its current state is substantially worse than rewinding.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Some rewinds are simple; this means rewinding only the last action completed or one currently in progress.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Any judge may perform a simple rewind of the most recent action of a game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">When performing a simple rewind of a player&#x27;s game action, rewind to before the action began, such as before a played card would be put on the chain. The player may then opt not to take the action, or take the action making different decisions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">For rewinds more complicated than a simple rewind, only the head judge or designated appeals judges (at high OPL) may authorize rewinding in this way.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.5.d.</div><div class="rule-text">To rewind:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Each individual action since the time of the error is reversed, starting with the most recent one.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Every action must be reversed in the correct sequence.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.d.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If the identity of a card involved in a rewind is non-public (e.g. a drawn card), a random card is chosen from possible candidates.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">701.5.d.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player learned the identity of a card in a deck, but did not draw or channel it, it should be shuffled into the random part of the deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">701.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Investigations</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Any penalty that results in disqualification automatically starts an investigation into that player and patterns of behavior that might impact their eligibility in future competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.6.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Investigations may also occur at a judge&#x27;s discretion following the application of a penalty if cheating is suspected. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.6.c.</div><div class="rule-text">The result of an investigation can be suspension of the player from Riftbound competitions.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">701.6.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Suspended players may not enter Riftbound competitions. If a suspended player is found to have registered for a competition, the organizer reserves the right to issue a disqualification at any time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">702.</div><div class="rule-text">Game Play Errors</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Overview</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A game play error is caused by incorrect and inaccurate play of the game that violates the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Repeated game play error [ Warnings ] can upgrade to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.1.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Except for 702.17, if a player commits the same exact category of game play error [ Warning ] 3 or more times in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.1.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At competitive OPL, if a player commits a total of 6 or more of any category of game play error [ Warning ] in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.1.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">At professional OPL, if a player commits a total of 5 or more of any category of game play error [ Warning ] in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.1.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Even if a player has not yet met the threshold to upgrade their penalties, a head judge may initiate a cheating investigation if they feel it is appropriate. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, penalties should generally be less harsh and rewinds may be applied even if the erroring player gains an information advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.1.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, penalties that require an opponent of the erroring player to make a choice are made at random by a judge when they can&#x27;t be avoided by a rewind.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Missed Trigger [ No Penalty ]: The accountable player for a triggered ability doesn&#x27;t demonstrate awareness of the trigger before the first time it would have an observable impact on the game.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">See 506 for definition of triggered abilities.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">After the initial point at which a triggered ability is missed, any further errors related to that trigger are considered a General Error instead. See 702.15 for more information about General Errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may not attempt to cause triggered abilities where an opponent is the accountable player to be missed by taking game actions or otherwise prematurely advancing the game, including through otherwise acceptable shortcuts.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.d.</div><div class="rule-text">A triggered ability is considered to have triggered even if it was subsequently missed. Effects that count or restrict the number of times that ability can trigger will count the missed one.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.e.</div><div class="rule-text">If a mandatory trigger is missed by its accountable player and missing that trigger is advantageous for them, this penalty is a [ Warning ] instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.2.f.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.2.f.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the trigger was missed during a previous turn, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.2.f.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, the next opponent in turn order of the player who missed the trigger chooses whether to resolve the trigger immediately or miss it altogether.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Forgetting to Score a Point [ No Penalty ]: A player is entitled to a point from conquering or holding a battlefield but fails to track it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">This does not apply to points that would be scored from card triggers such as Ahri, Alluring.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.3.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the correct score can be determined and no more than a full round cycle has passed, increment the appropriate player&#x27;s score to that total.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.3.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Forgetting to Draw Cards [ No Penalty ]: A player is obligated to draw a card, either as part of their draw phase or instruction from a card, but fails to do so.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.4.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the missed draws can be determined and no more than a full round cycle has passed, the appropriate player draws the missing cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.4.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If forgetting to draw was advantageous to the erroring player (example: the player is near burning out), this penalty is a [ Warning ] instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Looking at Extra Cards [ Warning ]: While taking a game action that allows a player to look at cards from a deck, they look at more cards than they are entitled to.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Cards being looked at from secret information such as a deck are added to a private information group while they are being looked at.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Once extra cards being looked at have touched a new private or secret group from their original source, they become part of that group. If the extra cards have not touched a new group, this is instead a Looking at Secret Information error. See 702.10. for more on Looking at Secret Information errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.5.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">For example, if a player plays a Stacked Deck and adds a fourth card to the private set of cards they are looking at, it is Looking at Extra Cards. If they correctly look at the top three, but knock the top card of their Main Deck over without adding it to the private group, it is Looking at Secret Information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies :</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.5.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught while the erroring player is looking at the extra cards, the next opponent in turn order of the erroring player looks at the private group and chooses the appropriate number of extra cards to shuffle back into the randomized portion of the appropriate deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.5.c.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If one or more of the cards has already been added to a new group of cards, the next opponent in turn order of the erroring player looks at that group and chooses the appropriate number of cards to return to the original group of cards being looked at. Then perform 702.5.c.1</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.5.c.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught at a later stage in the game, instruct players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Channeling Too Many Runes [ Warning ]: A player channels a number of runes greater than they are entitled to under the Core Rules as the result of a limited action.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors of this sort are generally public, and all players are expected to be mindful of game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.6.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies :</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.6.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the additional runes have been used to pay for actions, rewind if possible. If rewinding is not possible, this is a General Error instead. See 702.15 for more information about General Errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.6.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the additional runes are caught and no more than a full round cycle has passed, shuffle them appropriately into the rune deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.6.b.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If the specific runes can&#x27;t be identified, shuffle random runes instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.6.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Channeling Too Few Runes [ No Penalty ]: A player channels a number of runes less than what they are entitled to under the Core Rules as the result of a limited action.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors of this sort are generally public, and all players are expected to be mindful of game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.7.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies :</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.7.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If no more than a full round cycle has passed, allow the player to channel the appropriate number of runes.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.7.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.7.c.</div><div class="rule-text">If forgetting to channel runes was advantageous to the erroring player, this penalty is a [ Warning ] instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Overpayment for Cost [ No Penalty ]: A player pays a cost overpaying its power or energy cost.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors of this sort are generally public, and all players are expected to be mindful of game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.8.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.8.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If a cost is overpaid and it is still the turn where the cost was paid, the overpaying player is considered to have the extra resources floating in their resource pool.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.8.b.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If overpaying a cost was advantageous to the erroring player, this penalty is a [ Warning ] instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.8.b.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If overpaying a cost caused a result that would not otherwise have happened, it is a General Error. See 702.15 for more information about General Errors.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Mispayment for Cost [ Warning ]: A player pays a cost underpaying its power or energy cost, or uses the incorrect domain to pay a power cost.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.9.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors of this sort are generally public, and all players are expected to be mindful of game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.9.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.9.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If possible, perform a simple rewind to before the card or ability was put on the chain.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.9.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If a simple rewind is not possible, correct the cost paid if possible, by exhausting, or recycling runes as needed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.9.b.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If runes were incorrectly recycled as part of the cost, rewind if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.9.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If rewinding is not possible, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.10.</div><div class="rule-text">Looking at Secret Information [ Warning ]: A player looks at information that is not available to any player. See CR 127. Privacy for information types.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.10.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players should not be penalized via a remedy for errors their opponents make. Example: If one of your opponents inappropriately flips over the top card of their deck, you do not receive a penalty if you see it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.10.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.10.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If possible, take involved cards in the now-known secret information and re-randomize them with the original randomized source of appropriate cards to remove the information advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.10.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If this is not possible, reveal the information to all players to remove the information advantage and instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.11.</div><div class="rule-text">Looking at Opponent Private Information [ Warning ]: A player accidentally looks at information that is private to their opponent. See CR 127. Privacy for information types.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.11.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players should not be penalized via a remedy for errors their opponents make. Example: If one of your opponents inappropriately drops a card from their hand on the table, you do not receive a penalty if you see it.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.11.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.11.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">The opponent whose private information it is may decide whether to take involved cards in the now-known private information and re-randomize them with the original randomized source of appropriate cards.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.11.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If they decide not to, instruct the players to proceed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.12.</div><div class="rule-text">Drawing Extra Cards [ Warning ]: A player draws more cards than they are entitled to under the Core Rules as the result of a limited action or a player draws cards when they are not entitled to under the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.12.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Drawing extra cards is also looking at extra cards, but only this error should be recorded.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.12.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.12.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught before the extra cards drawn are added to a private set, shuffle them into the main deck appropriately to remove the information advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.12.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught when a player draws their opening hand during the start of game procedure and the player has not performed their mulligan yet, shuffle the extra cards into the main deck appropriately to remove the information advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.12.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught after the cards drawn are added to a private set and it is within the round cycle, the next opponent in turn order of the erroring player looks at that player&#x27;s hand and chooses the appropriate number of cards to shuffle into the main deck appropriately.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.12.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If at a later stage in the game it is determined a player has more cards in hand than they should, the remedy should be applied at this time.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.13.</div><div class="rule-text">Facedown Card Error [ Warning ]: A player places a card into a facedown zone in a manner other than how they are entitled to under the Core Rules.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.13.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: Hiding a card that does not have the Hidden keyword.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.13.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.13.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught by the erroring player immediately and before any additional game actions have been taken, return the card to the zone it came from and resume play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.13.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught after additional game actions have been taken but before any cards have been added to the zone where the illegal card came from (such as a new card being drawn), return the illegal card to that zone and replace it with a random card from that zone that is legal for the facedown zone and resume play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.13.b.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If there is no available legal replacement, this penalty upgrades to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.13.b.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught after cards have been added to the zone where the illegal card came from, this penalty upgrades to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.14.</div><div class="rule-text">Mulligan Procedure Error [ Warning ]: A player performs the mulligan procedure incorrectly.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.14.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.14.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player recycled and replaced more than 2 cards during their mulligan, the next opponent in turn order of the erroring player looks at that player&#x27;s hand and may choose cards up to the number of extra cards mulliganed to have that player recycle and replace.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">702.14.a.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: If a player accidentally mulliganed 3 cards, the next opponent in turn order would look at that player&#x27;s hand and may choose up to one card to have that player recycle and replace.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.14.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If this error is caught after gameplay has begun, instruct the players to proceed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.15.</div><div class="rule-text">Forgetting to Ready During the Awaken Phase [ No Penalty ]: A player forgets to ready one or more game objects during their Awaken Phase.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.15.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.15.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If it is still the turn the erroring player forgot to awaken, ready all game objects that should have been readied. If a game object being readied this way would put a trigger on the chain, it is not put on the chain instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.15.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If it is no longer the turn the erroring player forgot to awaken, leave game objects in their current state and proceed with play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.15.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If forgetting to ready was advantageous to the erroring player, this penalty is a [ Warning ] instead.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.16.</div><div class="rule-text">General Error [ Warning ]: Any other violation of the Rules of Riftbound .</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.16.a.</div><div class="rule-text">This is a catch-all for any other game play error.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.16.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Errors of this sort are generally public, and all players are expected to be mindful of game state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.16.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.16.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Rewind if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">702.16.c.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Otherwise, instruct the players to continue playing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">702.17.</div><div class="rule-text">Failure to Maintain Game State [ Warning ]: A player does not call out another player&#x27;s failure to maintain the proper game state, including missed scoring from hold and conquer.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.17.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Generally this error occurs when a player&#x27;s opponent commits a game play error and the player doesn&#x27;t call a judge immediately.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">702.17.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Unlike other Game Play Errors, this error is primarily for tracking and never upgrades unless specifically instructed by the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">703.</div><div class="rule-text">Tournament Errors</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Overview</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">A tournament error is caused by actions in or around a tournament match that violate tournament procedure expectations.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Repeated tournament error [ Warnings ] can upgrade to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.1.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player commits the same category of tournament error [ Warning ] 3 or more times in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.1.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">At competitive OPL, if a player commits a total of 6 or more of any category of tournament error [ Warning ] in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.1.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">At professional OPL, if a player commits a total of 5 or more of any category of tournament error [ Warning ] in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.1.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Even if a player has not yet met the threshold to upgrade their penalties, a head judge may initiate a cheating investigation if they feel it is appropriate. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">At low OPL, penalties should generally be less harsh and rewinds may be applied even if the erroring player gains an information advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.1.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Penalties that require an opponent of the erroring player to make a choice can be made at random or by a judge if they can&#x27;t be avoided by a rewind.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Slow Play [ Warning ]: A player is performing their start of game procedure or is playing mid-game at an unreasonably slow pace.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Players must take their turns in a timely manner, appropriate to a judge according to the state of the game at the time, and adhere to time limits specified for the tournament.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.2.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may ask a judge to watch their game for slow play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.2.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Intentionally stalling to gain an advantage is cheating. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Decklist Error [ Game Loss ]: A player submits a decklist for registration that contains illegal elements.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">More or fewer cards than appropriate deck size. See 402 for more information about deck size.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Cards not legal in the format of the tournament.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">More than 3 copies of a main deck card between main deck and sideboard.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Cards that do not match the domain of the registered legend.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.5.</div><div class="rule-text">A chosen champion that does not match the identity of the legend.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.a.6.</div><div class="rule-text">A player&#x27;s decks contain cards that do not match their registered decklist.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player&#x27;s registered decklist is illegal, the player must correct their decklist to a legal state making as little change as possible and ensure that their deck matches the newly registered deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player&#x27;s presented decks do not match their registered decklist but their presented decks are legal, the player must change their registered decklist to match their presented deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.3.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If the cards required to correct a decklist cannot be acquired in a timely manner, the head judge can issue a proxy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Deck Presentation Error [ Warning ]: A player&#x27;s deck&#x27;s contents are illegal during presentation to an opponent or during gameplay.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A player submits a battlefield they have already used in the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A player forgets to restore their deck to a pre-sideboard state for the first game of a match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">A player forgets to take their chosen champion from their main deck and place it in the chosen champion zone.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">A player&#x27;s deck is the incorrect deck size. See 402 for more information about deck size. (Examples: A player misplaced a card, a player forgot to shuffle their banished cards back into their deck between games, a player&#x27;s deck has an extra card or is missing a card due to an effect like Possession from a previous game.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.5.</div><div class="rule-text">A player has cards in their deck box with their deck that are not part of the registered contents of their decklist. Promotional cards given out as part of the event, tokens, and damaged cards replaced with official proxies are allowed to be stored this way and do not warrant this error.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.a.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At Professional OPL, extra cards stored in the deck box this way upgrades to a [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught by any player before opening hands have been drawn, restore the deck to its correct state if possible and instruct players to proceed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught by the erroring player after opening hands have been drawn and it is easily possible, restore the deck to its correct state and instruct players to proceed. For example:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">In game one of a match a player realizes they forgot to restore their deck to a pre-sideboard state.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.2.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A judge can restore their deck to the appropriate state by returning cards to the main deck and sideboard as needed.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-6"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.2.a.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If sideboard cards are in the player&#x27;s hand, they are replaced randomly by main deck cards that were incorrectly in the sideboard.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-5"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.2.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Shuffle the randomized portion of the main deck once this process is complete.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.3.</div><div class="rule-text">If the error is caught by a player other than the erroring player after opening hands have been drawn, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">At casual or competitive OPL, if the error is a player forgetting to move their Chosen Champion from their Main Deck to the chosen champion zone at setup, the judge may downgrade the penalty to a [ Warning ] and restore the gamestate if possible, regardless of who caught the error or at which stage of the game it occurred.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may request a judge check an opponent&#x27;s deck for legality. (Example: A player believes an opponent has a sideboard card in the first game.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.4.</div><div class="rule-text">If restoring the deck to its correct state is not possible, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">When applying a remedy for a missing Chosen Champion is applicable, if it occurs at any point after opening hands have been drawn and no copies of the Chosen Champion are available in the Main Deck to move to the Chosen Champion zone to restore the gamestate, the penalty may be downgraded to a [ Warning ] and gameplay can continue without the erroring player having a champion in the Chosen Champion zone.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-4"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.4.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Example: A player has two total copies of Traveling Merchant on their registered decklist, one in their Main Deck and one in their Sideboard. During game one of a match a player draws and plays one Traveling Merchant. On a later turn, they draw the second copy and realize they forgot to de-sideboard. At this point, restoring the deck is not possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.4.b.5.</div><div class="rule-text">If the cards required to correct the deck cannot be acquired in a timely manner, the head judge can issue a proxy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Communication Violation [ Warning ]: A player does not effectively communicate with their opponents during gameplay.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">This error includes any unintentional violation of the communication policies listed in section 500.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.5.b.1.</div><div class="rule-text">If any incorrect information was communicated, the correct information is supplied.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.5.b.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If any decisions were made based on incorrect communication, perform a rewind if possible.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Marked Cards [ Warning ]: A player&#x27;s cards are possible to identify without seeing their face.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.6.a.</div><div class="rule-text">This includes but is not limited to scratches, discoloration, and bends.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.6.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player&#x27;s cards are sleeved, the cards must be examined while in the sleeves to determine if they are marked.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.6.c.</div><div class="rule-text">The Head Judge has the authority to determine if a card in a player&#x27;s deck is marked.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.6.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.6.d.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Judges may request that a player remove their current sleeves or replace any of the deck&#x27;s current sleeves or cards immediately, or before the next round.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.6.d.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If the cards required to correct the deck cannot be acquired in a timely manner, the head judge can issue a proxy.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Insufficient Randomization [ Warning ]: A player did not sufficiently randomize their deck when required to.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.7.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player believes an opponent has not sufficiently randomized their Main Deck or Rune Deck before presenting them, they may call a judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.7.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Pile shuffling is not a sufficient randomization method and should only be used as a counting method.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.7.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Remedies:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.7.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">A judge may instruct a player to rerandomize their deck.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">703.7.c.2.</div><div class="rule-text">A judge may randomize the player&#x27;s deck for them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Late to Match: A player arrives at their assigned match after the round clock has started.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player arrives within 2 minutes of the round start, the penalty is a [ Warning ] and a time extension is issued.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.8.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player arrives within 10 minutes of the round start, the penalty is a [ Warning ] and a time extension is issued. At high OPL the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.8.c.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player arrives after 10 minutes of the round start, the penalty is a [ Match Loss ].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.8.d.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player never arrives for their match, the penalty is a [ Match Loss ] and the player is dropped from the tournament unless they report to a judge before the next match begins.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.8.e.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may call a judge before the round clock starts or in between games of a match to request up to a 10 minute time extension in lieu of penalty at the judge&#x27;s discretion. (Example: A player requests to use the restroom.)</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.9.</div><div class="rule-text">Outside Assistance: A player receives advice or strategic assistance from an individual outside of the match.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.a.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player receiving the advice actively refuses the advice by calling a judge, they receive a [ Warning ] . Otherwise, that player receives a [ Match Loss ] . This penalty does not apply if the head judge deems the advice was delivered maliciously.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If the player receiving the advice solicited the advice, it is cheating. See 704.8 for more information about cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.c.</div><div class="rule-text">If the spectator giving the outside assistance is also a player in the event, this penalty is also applied to them.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.d.</div><div class="rule-text">Spectators providing advice may be barred from the tournament venue if the advice was given maliciously or knowingly.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.e.</div><div class="rule-text">It is not outside assistance to receive strategic advice from a teammate in the 2v2 format or in other team formats when specified.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.9.f.</div><div class="rule-text">At a judge&#x27;s discretion, a player may receive assistance with the physical logistics of the competition, including game actions such as shuffling or card movement. This is not considered outside assistance.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">703.10.</div><div class="rule-text">Improperly Determining a Winner [ Match Loss ]: Games and matches of Riftbound must be decided by playing Riftbound appropriate to the tournament format.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.10.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Other methods, including but not limited to flipping a coin, rolling dice, or playing other games, may not be used to determine a winner.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.10.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Playing other formats of Riftbound other than the one designated for the tournament with the appropriate registered decklist may not be used to determine a winner.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">703.10.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Offering or accepting such offers is considered improperly determining a winner.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-0"><div class="rule-id">704.</div><div class="rule-text">Unsporting Conduct</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Overview</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.1.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Riftbound does not tolerate unsporting conduct. Tournament attendees and spectators must behave in a respectful and honorable manner.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.1.b.</div><div class="rule-text">If a player commits the same category of unsporting conduct [ Warning ] 2 or more times in the same event, the penalty is upgraded to [ Game Loss ] and may be further upgraded at the discretion of the head judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.1.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Infractions and penalties listed in this section aim to serve as general guidelines. Listed infractions are examples and are not exhaustive. All judges are empowered to escalate unsporting conduct issues and the head judge may apply any penalty they deem appropriate for unsporting conduct, up to and including [Disqualification].</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Unsporting Minor [ Warning ]:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.2.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples include but are not limited to:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.2.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Disrespecting attendees or spectators</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.2.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Bullying</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.2.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Poor hygiene</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.2.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Offensive play materials</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Unsporting Major [Disqualification]:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.3.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples include but are not limited to:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.3.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Use of racial slurs</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.3.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Physical violence or the threat of physical violence</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.3.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Displaying aggression</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-heading level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.3.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Intentionally misgendering an event attendee</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.3.b.</div><div class="rule-text">This penalty often comes with ejection from the venue at the discretion of tournament officials.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Angle Shooting [ Warning ]: A player uses the technical precision of the rules to gain an advantage where the spirit of the rules would not have garnered that advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Examples include but are not limited to:</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Asking a player to state a choice that they would make on resolution prior to deciding whether to react.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Taking actions quickly to prevent opponents from having time to have priority.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Promising a concession to garner additional information before making a different decision.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.4.</div><div class="rule-text">Using a printing of a card that is pre-errata without informing opponents once the card is played.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Baiting an opponent into a rules violation.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Intentionally distracting an opponent to get them to miss their priority window.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.4.a.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Using sloppy play or game state representation to obscure strategy or information.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.5.</div><div class="rule-text">Bribery [ Match Loss ]: Tournament attendees and spectators may not bribe tournament players or tournament officials.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.5.a.</div><div class="rule-text">The outcome of tournament games may not be influenced by offers of out-of-game rewards or incentives.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.5.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Making or accepting such an offer is considered bribery.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.5.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">Bribery applies to actions within a game, not just the final results.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.5.a.3.</div><div class="rule-text">Receiving an offer is not considered bribery unless entertained or accepted, but the receiving player must inform a judge.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.5.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Sharing prizes that have not yet been awarded in the current tournament does not constitute bribery. Players are permitted to make such agreements before or during their match, provided that the prize sharing is not offered or accepted in return for the outcome of a game or match, any in-game action, or a player dropping from the tournament.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.5.c.</div><div class="rule-text">It is not bribery when players in the single-elimination portion of a tournament offering only items such as cash, store credit, and/or unopened product, with the tournament organizer&#x27;s permission, agree to divide the subsequent tournament prizes evenly. All players still in the tournament must agree to the arrangement.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.5.c.1.</div><div class="rule-text">Players may end their participation in the tournament at this point or continue to play. If this is a major streamed event, players must continue to play for final rankings.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.5.d.</div><div class="rule-text">It is not bribery when, in the final round of the single-elimination portion of a tournament, with the tournament organizer&#x27;s permission, players mutually agree on a winner and how to divide the remaining prizes. In such cases, one player must agree to drop from the tournament. Prizes are then awarded based on each player&#x27;s final standing.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.6.</div><div class="rule-text">Wagering [Disqualification]: Tournament attendees may not wager on any portion of a tournament, match, or game, including its outcome.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.7.</div><div class="rule-text">Theft [Disqualification]: Stealing or attempting to steal the property of a tournament attendee, spectator, vendor, or event organizer.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-1"><div class="rule-id">704.8.</div><div class="rule-text">Cheating [Disqualification]: Riftbound does not tolerate cheating at any level of play.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.8.a.</div><div class="rule-text">Cheating includes but is not limited to performing any action that results in a penalty listed under 702 or 703 if it is done knowingly or maliciously to obtain an advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.8.a.1.</div><div class="rule-text">This includes attempting to create game errors to use a remedy to the player&#x27;s advantage.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-3"><div class="rule-id">704.8.a.2.</div><div class="rule-text">If judges suspect a player is violating the rules knowingly or maliciously, the head judge may initiate an investigation to determine if cheating occurred.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.8.b.</div><div class="rule-text">Lying to a tournament official is cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.8.c.</div><div class="rule-text">Intentionally neglecting to indicate a teammate&#x27;s error is cheating.</div></div>

<div class="rule-row rule-plain level-2"><div class="rule-id">704.8.d.</div><div class="rule-text">The head judge has ultimate discretion to issue penalties for cheating.</div></div>
